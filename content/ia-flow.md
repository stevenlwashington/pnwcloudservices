# Information Architecture (IA) Flow — PNW Cloud Services

This document outlines the user flows, page relationships, and intent-driven navigation paths.

---

## 1. Primary Entry Points

Users typically arrive via:

- Home (`/`)
- Blog (`/blog`)
- Direct LinkedIn posts linking to blog or service pages
- Search (future SEO)

---

## 2. Main Flows by Persona

### Flow A — Platform Modernization Buyer
1. Home  
2. Scroll through stats + services  
3. Open Platform Modernization  
4. Review diagrams, UX, case study  
5. CTA → Contact

Flow: `/` → `/platform-modernization` → `/contact`

---

### Flow B — RevOps / GTM Systems Leader
1. Header → Services → Salesforce & CRM Strategy  
2. Scan CRM architecture, integrations, forecasting, compliance  
3. CTA → Contact

Flow: `/salesforce-crm-strategy` → `/contact`

---

### Flow C — Engineering Leader (DevEx / DevOps)
1. Home or LinkedIn  
2. DevEx & DevOps  
3. Pipeline diagram, toolchain, DORA outcomes  
4. Related link → Observability  
5. CTA → Contact

Flow: `/devex-devops` → `/observability` → `/contact`

---

### Flow D — AI Decision-Maker
1. Blog or LinkedIn link  
2. AI Automation page  
3. Capability matrix, LLM architecture, tooling  
4. CTA → Contact

Flow: `/blog/[slug]` → `/ai-automation` → `/contact`

---

### Flow E — Compliance Stakeholder
1. Footer → Compliance & Governance  
2. TCPA/CPRA content  
3. CTA → Contact

Flow: `/compliance-governance` → `/contact`

---

## 3. Cross-Linking Strategy

- Every service page includes:
  - Relevant case studies  
  - A CTA at bottom  
  - 1–2 cross-links to related services  

---

## 4. Secondary Navigation Pathways

- Footer repeats all service links  
- Blog posts link to related services  
- Mobile nav mirrors desktop structure
